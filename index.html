About Me

First Generation Armenian American College Graduate

I think who you are tomorrow starts with what you do today. Values taught in my family growing up with nothing much and sharing a room with my Grandma most of my life. But with perseverance through laughs, hardships, losses, and blessings, I have learned to live by those values by heart.

I think a lot of it came while learning how hard the grind is in life. I found myself getting a job working stockroom at a retail store at 3:00A.M. in the morning so I can have open availability to grab the classes I needed in college. I later found myself working 40 hours a week there while being a full time student to bring to save up a little extra which ended up being more of a hindrance on myself than anything else.

I believe in a safe and friendly team environment. I have been in the situation in teams where I am either acted as the team leader, or the person trying to pitch in and help in any way he can. Tasks should be assigned with the intention of being accomplished. Any unfinished tasks should be reviewed during team meeting and assignments should be adjusted accordingly, whether that means shifting ones position to fit them better or providing extra support in that area.
